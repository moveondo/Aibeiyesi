A protein that is made by B cells and plasma cells (types
of white blood cells). Some immunoglobulins may be
found in higher than normal amounts in patients with
certain conditions or certain types of cancer, including
multiple myeloma and Waldenstrom macroglobulinemia.
Measuring the amount of specific immunoglobulins in
the blood and urine may help diagnose cancer or find out
how well treatment is working or if cancer has come
back. Some immunoglobulins may be used as tumor
